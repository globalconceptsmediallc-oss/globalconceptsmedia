Common leaks we find

The problem is usually not “more SEO.” It is finding what is blocking growth.

Before adding more pages, more keywords, or more reports, we look for the places where search visibility, trust, and conversions are breaking down.

Broken tracking

Calls, forms, ecommerce events, or lead sources are missing, duplicated, or not tied back to business outcomes.

Wrong pages ranking

The pages showing up in search are not always the pages most likely to turn visitors into customers.

Thin service pages

Important services are often buried, too generic, missing local intent, or not strong enough to earn trust.

Internal linking gaps

Strong pages do not perform when the site structure fails to pass authority to the pages that matter.

Indexing issues

Google may be seeing duplicates, redirects, old URLs, missing canonicals, or pages that should not compete.

Conversion bottlenecks

Traffic is not enough if the page does not create trust, urgency, clarity, or a strong next step.

Why most SEO fails

More traffic means very little if visitors never become calls, form fills, customers, booked jobs, store visits, or revenue. That is why we start with the leak before adding more activity.
