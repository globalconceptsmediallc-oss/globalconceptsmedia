Real examples

Leaks we found and fixed.

These examples are anonymous where needed. The point is not name-dropping. The point is showing the business problems we uncover and the work required to fix them.

Case Study 01

Merchant Center Recovery

Product visibility was being limited because catalog data and Merchant Center issues were preventing products from showing properly.

What We Found

Products were disappearing from Google Shopping because feed issues, product data gaps, and policy warnings were limiting visibility.

What We Fixed

We reviewed feed diagnostics, corrected product data, cleaned up Merchant Center issues, and monitored product eligibility.

What Changed

Product eligibility improved, shopping visibility began returning, and the store had a clearer path to compete for product searches.

Case Study 02

Analytics Recovery

The business could not trust its reporting because website traffic and conversion activity were not being measured correctly.

What We Found

Analytics tracking was missing, incomplete, duplicated, or not firing in the places where important business activity happened.

What We Fixed

We audited the live site, verified tags, corrected the installation path, tested events, and confirmed reporting through live validation.

What Changed

Reporting confidence was restored, conversion visibility improved, and decisions could be made from real data instead of guesswork.

Case Study 03

Technical SEO Recovery

Search visibility was being weakened by crawl confusion, broken URL patterns, redirects, and indexation problems.

What We Found

Authority was leaking through redirect loops, duplicate URL paths, bad canonicals, sitemap conflicts, broken pages, and crawl traps.

What We Fixed

We cleaned up redirects, normalized canonical signals, aligned the sitemap, repaired internal links, and reduced crawl confusion.

What Changed

Search engines had a cleaner path through the site, authority was better protected, and site health became easier to maintain.

Case Study 04

Local Visibility Recovery

A local business needed stronger city-level visibility and a clearer service structure for customers and search engines.

What We Found

Service pages and city pages were not working together clearly enough, leaving search engines unsure which locations and services mattered most.

What We Fixed

We expanded local page structure, strengthened service-to-city linking, improved crawl paths, and clarified geographic relevance.

What Changed

The site gained stronger local signals, better service coverage, and clearer visibility opportunities in target markets.

Case Study 05

Lead Generation Recovery

Paid traffic was active, but the path from visitor to qualified lead was unclear and difficult to measure.

What We Found

Campaigns were spending money without enough clarity around search intent, lead quality, conversion actions, and landing page performance.

What We Fixed

We reviewed campaign structure, cleaned up conversion goals, improved tracking, reviewed search terms, and clarified the lead path.

What Changed

Wasted spend was reduced, lead quality became easier to evaluate, and optimization decisions became more confident.

Why most agencies miss it

Most agencies report the symptoms. We look for the leak.

They look at reports.

Reports show what happened. They do not always explain why it happened. We look underneath the report to find the system issue.

They optimize ads.

Ads cannot be optimized properly if tracking, landing pages, calls, forms, and lead quality are not being validated.

They chase rankings.

Rankings matter, but traffic leaks, broken pages, weak internal links, and crawl issues can quietly hold a site back.

They report activity.

Activity is not the same as progress. We document what was done, why it mattered, and what changed.

They skip diagnostics.

Without diagnosis, marketing becomes guessing. Our process starts by finding the actual leak before recommending the fix.

They miss business context.

More traffic is not always the goal. Better leads, cleaner data, stronger visibility, and clearer decisions matter more.
