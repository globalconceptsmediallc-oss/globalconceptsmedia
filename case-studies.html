What We Found

The leak is usually hidden inside the system.

Most growth problems are not obvious from the outside. They show up when someone audits the full path from search to click to lead to sale.

Tracking gaps

Analytics tags missing, duplicate tags firing, events not recording, or conversions not matching what the business actually values.

Traffic leaks

Broken pages, weak internal links, missing redirects, bad canonical tags, outdated pages, or confusing crawl paths.

Lead quality problems

Ads bringing the wrong visitors, forms not being measured, calls not being tracked, or campaigns optimizing toward weak signals.

Product visibility issues

Product feeds, Merchant Center settings, policy warnings, missing data, or catalog issues limiting discoverability.

Reporting blind spots

Dashboards showing numbers without explaining what changed, why it changed, or what action should happen next.

Local authority gaps

Service pages and city pages not working together, leaving search engines unsure which locations and services matter most.

Why most agencies miss it

Most agencies report the symptoms. We look for the leak.

A ranking drop, weak lead quality, missing revenue, or bad campaign result is usually a symptom. The real problem may be tracking, product data, page structure, internal links, redirects, forms, calls, or the way the business is measuring success.

That is why our process starts with diagnosis before execution.
